About Nairobi

Kenya's vibrant capital and economic hub

Introduction

Nairobi is the capital and largest city of Kenya. The name comes from the Maasai phrase Enkare Nairobi, which translates to "cool water", a reference to the Nairobi River which flows through the city. Founded in 1899 as a railway depot, Nairobi has grown to become a cosmopolitan metropolis of over 4 million people.

Nairobi is known as the "Green City in the Sun" due to its lush tree-lined streets and pleasant climate. It's the only capital city in the world that contains a national park within its boundaries, offering a unique blend of urban development and natural wildlife.

History

Nairobi was established in 1899 by the British colonial authorities in British East Africa as a rail depot on the Uganda Railway. The town quickly grew to replace Mombasa as the capital of Kenya in 1907. After independence in 1963, Nairobi became the capital of the Republic of Kenya.

During Kenya's colonial period, the city became a center for the colony's coffee, tea and sisal industry. Nairobi also served as the capital of the British East Africa Protectorate, which attracted many settlers from Britain and South Africa.

In recent decades, Nairobi has become an important commercial and cultural hub in Africa. It hosts many multinational companies and organizations, including the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) and the United Nations Human Settlements Programme (UN-Habitat), giving it the nickname "The Safari Capital of the World".

Economy

Nairobi is the economic hub of Kenya and East Africa. The city contributes approximately 60% of Kenya's total GDP. Major industries include finance, manufacturing, technology, and tourism. Nairobi is home to the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE), one of the largest stock exchanges in Africa.

The city has established itself as a technology hub for Africa, with the growth of "Silicon Savannah" - a concentration of tech startups and innovation centers. Major international tech companies including Google, Microsoft, and IBM have established their African headquarters in Nairobi.

Culture

Nairobi boasts a vibrant cultural scene with numerous museums, galleries, and theaters. The National Museum of Kenya, the Nairobi Gallery, and the Railway Museum offer insights into the country's history and heritage. The city hosts several cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Nairobi International Cultural Festival and the Koroga Festival.

Nairobi's cuisine reflects its diverse population, with traditional Kenyan dishes like nyama choma (grilled meat) and ugali (maize porridge) served alongside international cuisine. The city's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous restaurants, clubs, and entertainment venues.

Nairobi is also a major sports hub in East Africa, hosting international rugby, cricket, and football matches. The city is home to the famous Kip Keino classic athletics meeting and has produced world-renowned athletes.
