Top Four Political Leaders of Nairobi

Current influential political figures shaping Nairobi's future

Johnson Sakaja

Governor of Nairobi County

Johnson Sakaja is the current Governor of Nairobi City County, elected in 2022. A graduate of the University of Nairobi with a degree in Actuarial Science, Sakaja entered politics as a nominated Member of Parliament in 2013. He served as a Senator for Nairobi County from 2017 to 2022 before his election as Governor.

Sakaja's administration has focused on improving Nairobi's infrastructure, addressing traffic congestion, and enhancing service delivery. He has initiated projects to decongest the city center, improve public transportation, and reclaim public spaces. His "Nairobi ya Kilele" vision aims to transform Nairobi into a world-class city.

Before entering politics, Sakaja was actively involved in youth leadership, serving as the Chairman of the National Youth Council. He has advocated for youth inclusion in governance and economic opportunities. As Governor, he has emphasized public-private partnerships to drive development in Nairobi.

Sakaja's administration faces challenges including managing the city's rapid urbanization, addressing housing shortages, and improving waste management. His approach combines technological innovation with community participation to find sustainable solutions for Nairobi's complex urban challenges.

Edwin Sifuna

Nairobi County Senator

Edwin Sifuna is the Senator for Nairobi County, elected in 2022. A lawyer by profession, Sifuna served as the Secretary-General of the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party before his election to the Senate. He is known for his vocal advocacy on county governance issues and oversight of the Nairobi County Government.

As Senator, Sifuna has focused on legislative matters affecting Nairobi residents, including county revenue allocation, urban planning laws, and devolution implementation. He has been instrumental in pushing for legislation to improve public service delivery in Nairobi and enhance accountability in county governance.

Sifuna's legal background has informed his approach to legislative work, with emphasis on constitutionalism and the rule of law. He has championed causes related to urban housing rights, environmental protection, and equitable development across Nairobi's constituencies.

Through his committee work in the Senate, Sifuna has addressed issues of county finance management, intergovernmental relations, and urban policy. He maintains an active presence in Nairobi's wards, engaging with residents to understand their needs and represent their interests at the national level.

Sifuna's political career builds on his experience in party administration and grassroots mobilization. He continues to advocate for stronger devolution and more resources for Nairobi to address its unique challenges as Kenya's capital city.

Mike Sonko

Former Governor of Nairobi County

Mike Mbuvi Sonko served as the Governor of Nairobi County from 2017 to 2020. Known for his flamboyant style and philanthropic activities, Sonko entered politics as a Member of Parliament for Makadara Constituency before becoming Senator for Nairobi County and later Governor.

During his tenure as Governor, Sonko initiated several projects including the construction of health facilities, road repairs, and social welfare programs. His administration faced challenges including impeachment proceedings and eventual removal from office by the Senate in 2020.

Sonko's political style was characterized by direct engagement with Nairobi residents, often responding personally to service delivery complaints through social media. He established a 24-hour emergency response team and launched several humanitarian initiatives targeting vulnerable populations in Nairobi.

Despite controversies during his administration, Sonko maintained significant popularity in certain Nairobi constituencies, particularly in informal settlements where his philanthropic activities had substantial impact. His political approach combined populist messaging with direct service delivery.

Since leaving office, Sonko has remained active in Nairobi's political scene through his foundation and continued engagement with grassroots organizations. His tenure as Governor highlighted both the challenges of managing Nairobi's complex governance structure and the potential for direct executive action in county administration.

Esther Passaris

Nairobi Women Representative

Esther Passaris is the Women Representative for Nairobi County, first elected in 2017 and re-elected in 2022. A businesswoman and philanthropist, Passaris founded the Adopt-A-Light initiative to improve street lighting in Nairobi before entering politics. She has focused on women's empowerment, environmental issues, and social welfare programs.

As Women Representative, Passaris has championed legislation and programs supporting gender equality, economic empowerment for women, and protection from gender-based violence. She has established several initiatives providing business training, healthcare access, and educational support for women and girls in Nairobi.

Passaris has been instrumental in advocating for better maternal healthcare services in Nairobi's public hospitals and promoting women's participation in leadership and decision-making. Her office has facilitated the establishment of several women-led enterprises and cooperatives across Nairobi's constituencies.

Beyond women-specific issues, Passaris has been active in environmental conservation efforts in Nairobi, including tree planting initiatives and advocacy for sustainable urban development. She has collaborated with various stakeholders to address challenges facing vulnerable populations in Nairobi, including persons with disabilities and youth.

Passaris combines her legislative role with continued philanthropic work through her foundation, addressing both immediate needs and systemic challenges facing Nairobi residents. Her approach emphasizes partnership with civil society, private sector, and community organizations to achieve sustainable development in Nairobi.
